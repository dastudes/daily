Pittsburgh 2, Chicago Cubs 1 is the kind of result that should worry Cubs fans, even in May. The Cubs have now lost nine in a row, and they've gone from comfortable to merely hanging on in the NL Central — a division where, as of this morning, every single team is above .500. That's not a typo. Five teams, all with winning records, separated by four and a half games. Pittsburgh's Henry Davis [1-3, 1 HR | RC 7] provided the decisive blow in the bottom of the seventh, and the Cubs wasted a perfectly respectable outing from Ben Brown [6 IP, 1 ER, 7 K | 2.04 ERA, PAR 19], who turned in one of the day's best pitching performances by PAR. The Brewers, meanwhile, kept their grip on first place by handling St. Louis behind Jacob Misiorowski [7 IP, 1 ER, 12 K | 1.83 ERA, PAR 29], whose evening at American Family Field was the most dominant pitching line of the day. Milwaukee's young right-hander has now made himself a legitimate Cy Young conversation piece in the National League.

Out west, the Dodgers continue to roll. Their win over Colorado was the third game on the dramatic-swing leaderboard, with the Rockies actually pushing into the seventh before Freddie Freeman [1-4 | RC 31] doubled home Shohei Ohtani [1-3, 1 BB] to break the game open. The Padres, on the other hand, had no answer for Jesús Luzardo [6 IP, 0 ER, 6 K | 4.41 ERA, PAR 16], who shut them out over six in the Phillies' road win at Petco. That dropped San Diego a full game in the NL West, where the Diamondbacks — winners of three straight after thumping the Giants at Oracle Park behind Ketel Marte [4-5 | RC 31] and Merrill Kelly [7 IP, 2 ER, 4 K | 5.25 ERA, PAR 4] — have quietly climbed within striking distance. Arizona has the look of a team that's figured something out.

The day's most theatrical game was in Baltimore, where the Orioles and Rays played thirteen innings and produced a 7.18 WPA swing — the largest of the day by a comfortable margin. Tampa Bay scored in the 11th, 12th, and 13th. Baltimore answered every time, and Colton Cowser [1-4, 1 HR | RC 9]'s two-run shot in the bottom of the 13th finally ended it. It doesn't change the AL East picture much — the Rays still lead the Yankees in the division — but it was the sort of game that, win or lose, leaves a bullpen in tatters for a week. The Yankees, for their part, had their own ninth-inning theatrics in Kansas City, where Anthony Volpe [1-3, 1 BB | RC 3] and Jazz Chisholm Jr. [1-3, 1 BB | RC 24] combined to flip a one-run deficit into a one-run lead in the top of the ninth.

A few individual notes worth pulling out:

  • James Wood [4-5, 1 HR | RC 45] of the Nationals had a four-hit day at Progressive Field, including his 14th home run, as Washington pummeled Cleveland.
  • Curtis Mead [2-5, 2 HR | RC 20] of the Nationals went deep twice in the same game. The Nationals hit five home runs in total.
  • Tatsuya Imai [6 IP, 0 ER, 2 K | 6.23 ERA, PAR 0] threw six no-hit innings at Globe Life Field as Houston shut out Texas. The walks (four of them) kept it from being a serious bid, but the Astros have now won four in a row and are creeping back into the AL West.
  • Kyle Schwarber [2-3, 1 BB, 1 HR | RC 42] hit his major-league-leading 21st home run.
  • Munetaka Murakami [1-4, 1 HR | RC 38] of the White Sox hit his AL-leading 18th in a win over Minnesota that pulled Chicago within striking distance of the Guardians.

A word on the NL East: Atlanta still has a comfortable lead, but the Braves lost and Washington, Philadelphia, and Miami all won. The Nationals are now eight and a half back — a real distance, but the kind of distance that's at least worth checking on the standings for. The Mets, who fell to Nick Lodolo [6 IP, 1 ER, 7 K | 5.57 ERA, PAR 0] and the Reds, have now lost four straight and are slipping out of view entirely. Cincinnati is doing this, by the way, with a team FIP north of 5.00 and a run differential of minus-29. Their pythagorean record says they should be 23-30. They're 28-25. Pick your explanation — bullpen sequencing, timely hitting, dumb luck — but at some point that gap tends to close.
