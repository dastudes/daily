The American League East continues to be a two-team affair, and the Yankees and Rays both did their part to keep it that way. New York buried Texas behind a six-run sixth inning capped by Trent Grisham [3-5 | RC 14]'s bases-clearing double, while Tampa Bay went into Fenway and put up eight on the Red Sox, the Rays' seventh straight win. Boston now sits ten back, and the gap between the top two and everyone else in this division — nine games to Baltimore — looks more like a canyon every day.

Out west, the AL West is doing the opposite of what AL East is doing: nobody wants the thing. The Athletics, of all teams, hold first place after thumping the Phillies behind eight innings of one-run ball from J.T. Ginn [8 IP, 1 ER, 8 K | 3.46 ERA, PAR 8], while Texas, Seattle, and the rest of the division all lost. Meanwhile in the NL Central — easily the deepest division in baseball, with five teams above .500 — the Cubs extended their winning streak to nine by handling Cincinnati, the Cardinals snuck past San Diego on Masyn Winn [1-4 | RC 15]'s seventh-inning triple, and the Pirates took down Arizona behind Mitch Keller [6 IP, 2 ER, 4 K | 1.36 ERA, PAR 16]. The Reds, losers of seven straight, are sliding fast.

The day's most dramatic finish came in Miami, where the Marlins walked off the Orioles in the ninth on a throwing error by Coby Mayo [2-4 | RC 6] that scored Joe Mack [1-1 | RC 1]. It's the sort of loss that lingers — Baltimore had taken the lead in the top half and handed it to the bullpen, and the bullpen handed it back. Washington's win over Minnesota was the day's other heavyweight, with Keibert Ruiz [3-4, 1 HR | RC 7] providing essentially all of it: three hits, four RBI, including a two-run double in the fifth and a solo homer in the seventh. The Mets-Rockies game wasn't really dramatic in the sense of late tension — Colorado broke it open with a four-run eighth — but Jake McCarthy [2-4, 1 HR | RC 12]'s grand slam off Craig Kimbrel [1 IP, 4 ER, 2 K | 7.78 ERA, PAR 0] is the kind of swing that shows up in a highlight reel.

  • Bobby Witt Jr. [4-4, 1 BB, 1 HR | RC 25] had the best offensive day in baseball in a losing effort, going 4-for-4 with a homer against Cleveland.
  • Keibert Ruiz drove in four and effectively beat Minnesota by himself.
