The Yankees keep doing Yankees things, and Saturday brought another reminder that the AL East might already be tilting their way. New York's win over Baltimore pushed them to 22-11 while dropping the Orioles three games under .500 and into the division basement — a remarkable place to find a team that entered the year with October aspirations. The Rays kept pace with a comfortable win over San Francisco, but Tampa Bay's run differential suggests they're outrunning their underlying numbers by a healthy margin. Toronto's blowout in Minnesota, meanwhile, was the Blue Jays' second straight win, though at 16-17 they're still trying to climb back into a race that's getting away from them quickly.

In the NL Central — which now has four teams above .500 — the Cubs and Cardinals both made statements. Chicago's shutout of Arizona was their fourth straight win, keeping them three back of Atlanta for the best record in baseball. St. Louis, somehow, has now won six straight, the latest being a one-run squeaker over the Dodgers that handed Los Angeles its fourth consecutive loss. The Cardinals are 20-13 despite a run differential of plus-three, which we'll get to. And out west, San Diego's shutout loss to the White [1-3 | RC 3] Sox was their fourth straight defeat, dropping them a half-game behind the Dodgers despite L.A.'s own slump.
