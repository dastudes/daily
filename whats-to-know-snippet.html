There's something faintly absurd about the National League Central, where five of five teams sit above .500 and the Cubs just keep winning anyway. Chicago's tenth straight victory came at the expense of Texas, with Seiya Suzuki [1-3, 2 BB, 1 HR | RC 22] providing the decisive blow and Ben Brown [4 IP, 0 ER, 3 K | 1.85 ERA, PAR 12] and Javier Assad [3.2 IP, 0 ER, 1 K | 6.00 ERA, PAR 2] combining to hold the Rangers to a single run. The Cubs now lead St. Louis by three and a half, and the Cardinals aren't going anywhere either — they steamrolled San Diego behind Michael McGreevy [6 IP, 0 ER, 9 K | 2.20 ERA, PAR 15]'s near-flawless work, a result that nudged the Padres two games back of the Dodgers in the West. Speaking of the Dodgers, they handled Atlanta in a game that quietly carried weight: it was Chris Sale [7 IP, 2 ER, 7 K | 2.20 ERA, PAR 17] versus a Dodger bullpen parade, and the bullpen won, with Freddie Freeman [1-4, 1 HR | RC 22] providing the cushion. The AL East, meanwhile, saw Boston shut out Tampa Bay at Fenway, which gains the Red Sox a game on the Rays but doesn't change the fact that they're still nine back of the Yankees — who got handled themselves in Milwaukee. And in the NL East, where Atlanta's lead remains absurd, Washington kept pace with a one-run win at Miami while the Mets quietly took down Arizona in extras, dropping the Diamondbacks further out of the NL West picture.

The Rockies-Phillies game in Philadelphia was the day's wildest ride — Colorado led, Philadelphia rallied with a five-run eighth capped by Justin Crawford [3-4, 1 BB, 1 HR | RC 16]'s game-tying homer off Brennan Bernardino [0.1 IP, 3 ER, 1 K | 3.60 ERA, PAR 4], and then the Rockies pushed across two in the eleventh to steal it. A 4.29 WPA swing tells you the win expectancy was passed around like a hot potato. Kansas City's walkoff over Detroit was nearly as good: down to their last out twice, the Royals tied it in the eighth on a Kyle Isbel [2-4 | RC 14] single that included a Wenceel Pérez [2-4 | RC 5] throwing error, then won it in the ninth on another Isbel single. Tigers reliever Kyle Finnegan [0 IP, 2 ER, 0 K | 1.57 ERA, PAR 6] failed to record an out and surrendered the lead entirely, which is an unhappy line to wear home. And while Seattle's 12-8 win over the White Sox wasn't dramatic in the traditional sense — neither team was ever buried — Luke Raley [2-5, 2 HR | RC 17]'s grand slam in the third and three-run homer in the seventh gave it the requisite chaos.

  • Dylan Cease [7 IP, 0 ER, 10 K | 2.59 ERA, PAR 19] of Toronto was the day's best pitcher by PAR, dispatching the Angels with ten strikeouts and not a single walk. He leads the AL in strikeouts and it isn't particularly close.
  • Jacob Misiorowski [6 IP, 0 ER, 11 K | 2.45 ERA, PAR 17] struck out eleven Yankees in six innings, which extends his lead atop the NL strikeout list and made Max Fried [6 IP, 5 ER, 5 K | 2.94 ERA, PAR 21] look ordinary in the process.
  • Hunter Goodman [4-5, 1 HR | RC 23] of Colorado had four hits including a homer in the marathon at Citizens Bank Park — the top LWTS performance of the day.
  • Luke Raley drove in seven for Seattle on two home runs, which is the kind of afternoon you remember even when nobody else does.
