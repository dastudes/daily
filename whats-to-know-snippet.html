Sunday in May, and the script writers were drunk again. Baltimore staged a three-run ninth off Kenley Jansen [1 IP, 3 ER, 0 K | 5.11 ERA, PAR 1] in the opener, the kind of demolition that ends with Colton Cowser [1-2, 1 HR | RC 7] rounding the bases and the Tigers staring at their cleats — then Detroit came back hours later and won the nightcap behind Troy Melton [5.2 IP, 1 ER, 3 K | 1.73 ERA, PAR 2], because baseball doesn't owe anyone a coherent narrative. The Orioles got their walkoff and a split. The Tigers got their split and a flight home. Somewhere in the wreckage, the AL East got half a game more interesting, which is to say still not very.

The day's defining performance happened in Anaheim, where a last-place team and a third-place team played a 2-1 game nobody will remember in October, except for the man on the mound. Los Angeles's Reid Detmers [8 IP, 1 ER, 14 K | 4.57 ERA, PAR 16] struck out fourteen Rangers, allowed a solo shot to Texas's Jake Burger [1-4, 1 HR | RC 24], and otherwise treated the Texas lineup like a tutorial level. Texas's MacKenzie Gore [6 IP, 1 ER, 7 K | 4.42 ERA, PAR 12] was nearly his equal — six innings, seven strikeouts, one run — and lost anyway when Justin Foscue [1-4 | RC 8] booted a ninth-inning force play and Donovan Walton trotted home. The Rangers have now lost three straight and slipped a game in the West. The Angels are still the Angels, but for one afternoon they had the best pitcher on the planet.

Up at Yankee Stadium, Tampa Bay's Drew Rasmussen [7 IP, 0 ER, 6 K | 2.78 ERA, PAR 17] matched zeros with New York's Ryan Weathers [7 IP, 0 ER, 4 K | 3.15 ERA, PAR 17] for seven innings of nothing, and then Aaron Judge [2-4, 1 HR | RC 41] did what Aaron Judge does to baseballs left over the plate in the ninth — a two-run walkoff off Kevin Kelly [1 IP, 2 ER, 0 K | 2.98 ERA, PAR 7] that pulled the Yankees within four and a half. The Rays still lead the East. They also still cannot hit Ryan Weathers, which is its own kind of scouting report.

Down in Miami, the Mets took Christian Scott [5.2 IP, 0 ER, 5 K | 3.23 ERA, PAR 7]'s shutout into the ninth, handed the ball to Devin Williams [0.1 IP, 4 ER, 0 K | 6.35 ERA, PAR 2], and watched the building fall down. Heriberto Hernández [2-2, 1 HR | RC 7] hit a walkoff grand slam. Four pitches, four runs, four games back of nothing in particular, because the Mets are now thirteen and a half out and the Marlins are a footnote in the East that the Braves are running away with — or were, until Washington's Foster Griffin [6 IP, 0 ER, 6 K | 3.63 ERA, PAR 14] silenced Atlanta over six innings and the Nationals stole a 2-1 game at Truist. Orlando Ribalta [0.2 IP, 0 ER, 1 K | 2.41 ERA, PAR 4] got Ronald Acuña Jr. [0-3, 2 BB | RC 19] to ground softly to first with the tying run ninety feet away. The Braves have lost two in a row. The lead is still nine.

The NL Central remains the only division in baseball where five teams are above .500 and nobody seems to know what to do about it. Pittsburgh's Mitch Keller [6 IP, 1 ER, 5 K | 3.65 ERA, PAR 17] outpitched Toronto's Dylan Cease [4.2 IP, 2 ER, 8 K | 3.05 ERA, PAR 22] at Rogers Centre, Spencer Horwitz [1-4, 1 BB, 1 HR | RC 24] and Oneil Cruz [1-4, 1 HR | RC 30] and Esmerlyn Valdez [1-3, 1 HR | RC 1] all homered, and the Pirates pulled within four and a half of Milwaukee — who got steamrolled at home by the Dodgers, Yoshinobu Yamamoto [7 IP, 1 ER, 3 K | 3.09 ERA, PAR 18], and a four-run fifth that featured a Kyle Tucker [2-5 | RC 27] triple and an Andy Pages [1-4, 1 HR | RC 32] homer. Five teams in the Central, separated by four and a half games. Somebody's going to have to be good eventually. So far the volunteers have not stepped forward.

Elsewhere, Cleveland's Parker Messick [5.2 IP, 0 ER, 6 K | 2.25 ERA, PAR 24] quietly carved up Philadelphia, the Guardians won their eighth in ten, and Travis Bazzana [3-4, 1 HR | RC 14] homered in the eighth to put it away. Arizona's Ryne Nelson [8 IP, 1 ER, 3 K | 4.65 ERA, PAR 8] went eight against Colorado at Chase Field with Corbin Carroll [4-4 | RC 41] and Ketel Marte [3-4 | RC 28] doing the heavy lifting, and the Diamondbacks won their eighth in ten as well. The Cubs lost their eighth in a row at Wrigley, the Astros pounded Shota Imanaga [6 IP, 7 ER, 6 K | 4.07 ERA, PAR 14] for seven runs in six, and Christian Walker [1-4, 1 HR | RC 35] hit the three-run shot that broke it open. Chicago was a first-place team a fortnight ago. Now they're looking up at the Cardinals and wondering where they left the keys.
