The Dodgers continue to make their case as the most dangerous team in baseball, and Thursday night at Dodger Stadium offered another exhibit. Los Angeles dispatched the Phillies behind seven innings of one-hit work from Justin Wrobleski [7 IP, 1 ER, 9 K | 2.89 ERA, PAR 20], whose afternoon ranks as the second-best pitching performance in baseball today. Philadelphia's Zack Wheeler [6 IP, 4 ER, 4 K | 2.29 ERA, PAR 15] was hardly bad — he simply ran into a buzzsaw of solo home runs from Shohei Ohtani [3-4, 1 HR], Freddie Freeman [1-4, 1 HR | RC 35], Max Muncy [1-3, 1 HR | RC 31], and Will Smith [1-3, 1 HR | RC 21]. Kyle Schwarber [1-4, 1 HR | RC 44] answered with one of his own, but a Dodgers lineup that homers four times against Wheeler is a Dodgers lineup that wins. The loss drops Philadelphia further behind Atlanta in an NL East race that increasingly looks like a one-team affair, with the Braves now ten games clear of every team in the division save the Phillies.

In the AL East, Tampa Bay and New York both kept pace, but neither was tested by a contender. The Rays nearly coughed up a seven-run seventh inning against the Angels before Nick Martinez [7 IP, 2 ER, 5 K | 1.63 ERA, PAR 26] — already among the league's ERA leaders — stabilized things with another strong start, while Yandy Díaz [2-5, 2 HR | RC 40] and Jonathan Aranda [2-4, 1 BB, 1 HR | RC 37] provided the power. The Yankees, meanwhile, hammered the Athletics early behind Ben Rice [4-5, 1 HR | RC 48], who continues to look like the AL's most surprising slugger, and Paul Goldschmidt [1-5, 1 HR | RC 19]'s three-run shot in the first. Carlos Rodón [6 IP, 1 ER, 3 K | 3.32 ERA, PAR 5] did the rest. Toronto's late rally past Baltimore — capped by Vladimir Guerrero Jr. [2-4 | RC 29]'s eighth-inning double off Yennier Cano [0.1 IP, 2 ER, 0 K | 2.81 ERA, PAR 7] — keeps the Blue Jays climbing back toward respectability, though eight games out is eight games out.

The day's two most dramatic finishes came in games involving teams sitting near the bottom of their divisions, but the drama was real. At Rate Field, the White Sox and Tigers went to the tenth tied, where Chicago's Miguel Vargas [2-5, 1 HR | RC 35] sent everyone home with a two-run shot off Drew Anderson [0.2 IP, 1 ER, 1 K | 3.64 ERA, PAR 10] — the highest-WPA play of the day across baseball. Troy Melton [7 IP, 1 ER, 1 K | 1.48 ERA, PAR 5] had spun seven excellent innings for Detroit only to watch the bullpen squander it. At PNC Park, Bryan Reynolds [1-3, 2 BB, 1 HR | RC 36] delivered a walk-off two-run homer in the ninth to lift the Pirates over the Twins, the second-biggest WPA swing of the night. Pittsburgh remains very much alive in a Central where five teams sit above .500 and the Brewers continue to pull away from all of them.

Out west, Seattle's win over Arizona produced the largest WPA swing of the day at over five, a back-and-forth affair in which the Diamondbacks pushed across a run in the top of the ninth only to lose in the tenth. Crawford homered twice and Arozarena delivered the walk-off double; the Mariners now share first place in the AL West, a division where not a single team plays above .500 ball. Arizona's loss, combined with San Diego's win over Washington — keyed by Jackson Merrill [1-4, 1 BB, 1 HR | RC 18]'s seventh-inning two-run shot off Mitchell Parker [2 IP, 1 ER, 1 K | 5.28 ERA, PAR 3] — keeps the Padres firmly in first place ahead of the Dodgers in an NL West where the top three are separated by five and a half games and the bottom two have already begun playing for next year.
