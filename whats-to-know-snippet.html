Philadelphia continues to claw toward respectability in the National League East, sweeping a pair from San Francisco at Citizens Bank Park. The Phillies moved to 12-19 after back-to-back walkoff wins, each decided in the final at-bat. Meanwhile, Detroit's 5-2 victory in Atlanta pulled the Tigers even at 16-16, keeping them within striking distance of Cleveland in the AL Central. The Braves' loss dropped them to 22-10, still comfortably atop the NL East but no longer quite as invincible as their run differential suggests.

The most dramatic game of the day played out in Philadelphia, where the Giants and Phillies needed 10 innings to settle their second straight nail-biter. San Francisco twice held leads and still carried a one-run advantage into the bottom of the ninth before Brandon Marsh [1-1 | RC 16] doubled and Kyle Schwarber [4-4, 1 BB, 1 HR | RC 25] drove him in with another double off Keaton Winn [2 IP, 1 ER, 0 K | 1.48 ERA, PAR 4]. In the 10th, Justin Crawford [1-4 | RC 11] ended it with an infield single. The previous game featured a similar script: Bryson Stott [1-4 | RC 6] tripled home Adolis García [2-4 | RC 14] in the ninth and Justin Crawford followed with the game-winning single against Ryan Walker [0.2 IP, 2 ER, 1 K | 4.43 ERA, PAR 3]. The combined 10-WPA-swing from these two games tells you everything about how tightly contested they were. Washington's 5-4 win at Citi Field also had late-inning fireworks, with CJ Abrams [2-3, 1 BB, 1 HR | RC 24] launching a two-run homer in the eighth to give the Nationals their winning margin.

Logan Webb [7 IP, 1 ER, 6 K | 4.30 ERA, PAR 10] of the Giants delivered the day's best pitching performance despite taking the loss in that first Philadelphia walkoff, working seven innings and allowing just one earned run. His counterpart Cristopher Sánchez [6.2 IP, 2 ER, 7 K | 2.92 ERA, PAR 15] nearly matched him. Framber Valdez [6 IP, 2 ER, 8 K | 3.37 ERA, PAR 12] continues settling into his new home in Detroit, striking out eight Braves over six innings. The Baltimore bullpen got a quality start from Chris Bassitt [6.2 IP, 1 ER, 7 K | 5.46 ERA, PAR 2] in the opener against Houston before the Astros returned the favor with an 11-5 rout in the nightcap. Yordan Alvarez [3-4, 1 BB, 1 HR | RC 40] homered and collected three hits in that second game, extending his league-leading OPS to 1.199.

Cincinnati's win over Colorado kept them tied with the Dodgers at 20-11, three games back of Atlanta in what's shaping up as the National League's most competitive division race. The Reds continue to outperform their run differential by nearly five wins, the largest positive gap in baseball. St. Louis extended its winning streak to four games with a 10-5 victory at Pittsburgh, moving to 18-13 despite allowing more runs than they've scored. The Cardinals and Reds represent opposite ends of the Pythagorean spectrum—one overperforming its peripherals, the other banking wins despite underlying metrics that suggest mediocrity.
