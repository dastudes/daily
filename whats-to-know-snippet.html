The Yankees took two from the Rays at Yankee Stadium on a day when neither club's offense did much of anything until the ninth. New York's Ryan Weathers [7 IP, 0 ER, 4 K | 3.15 ERA, PAR 17] and Tampa Bay's Drew Rasmussen [7 IP, 0 ER, 6 K | 2.78 ERA, PAR 17] both turned in the kind of starts that used to be ordinary and now get treated like museum pieces — Weathers through seven, Rasmussen through seven, neither man rattled. Then Aaron Judge [2-4, 1 HR | RC 41] ended it with one swing off Kevin Kelly [1 IP, 2 ER, 0 K | 2.98 ERA, PAR 7]. That's a ballgame. No four-man parade through the eighth inning, no specialist for every hitter. Two starters who knew their job, and the best hitter in the league finishing it. The Rays still sit atop the East, but they had a chance to bury this one and didn't.

Down in the NL East, Washington's Foster Griffin [6 IP, 0 ER, 6 K | 3.63 ERA, PAR 14] went into Truist Park and shut Atlanta down for six. The Braves clawed one back in the ninth and had the tying run on with Ronald Acuña Jr. [0-3, 2 BB | RC 19] at the plate — and Orlando Ribalta [0.2 IP, 0 ER, 1 K | 2.41 ERA, PAR 4] got him to roll over, then struck out Chadwick Tromp [0-1 | RC 1] to end it. Atlanta still has the best record in the National League, but a loss like that, at home, to a team they ought to handle, is the kind of thing you remember in September. The Nats manufactured what they needed. The Braves didn't.
