The Tigers' victory in Atlanta wasn't just another interleague win — it kept Detroit at .500 and within striking distance in a muddled AL Central, while dropping the Braves to 22-10 and momentarily halting what's been the National League's most impressive run this season. Framber Valdez [6 IP, 2 ER, 8 K | 2.69 PAR] held the Braves in check through six innings before the bullpen took over, and when Matt Vierling [3-5, 2 RBI, 2 R] delivered a go-ahead double in the eighth, it gave Detroit its fourth win in its last six games after a rough mid-April stretch. The Orioles' split with Houston was equally consequential: Baltimore's blowout win in the first game pulled them closer to .500, but the Astros' rout in the nightcap kept Houston from falling further below .375 despite their run differential issues. For a Baltimore team that's underperformed its talent, taking one of two against a struggling division opponent might not feel like progress, but it at least arrested a slide that threatened to bury them early.

Philadelphia staged back-to-back walkoff wins against San Francisco, and the drama wasn't close between the two games — the Thursday contest produced a WPA swing north of six, making it the most dramatic result of the day. Down two runs in the ninth, Bryson Stott [1-4, 1 RBI, 1 R] tripled home the tying run before Justin Crawford [3-4] singled him home to end it, then the Phillies manufactured another ninth-inning rally Friday to complete the improbable sweep of a doubleheader that wasn't scheduled as such. The Giants have now lost three straight, and both defeats featured Logan Webb [7 IP, 1 ER, 6 K | 2.57 PAR] pitching well enough to win but getting nothing from the offense late. The Nationals-Mets game in Queens carried its own late-game tension, with CJ Abrams [2-3, 1 BB, 1 HR, 3 RBI] delivering a two-run homer in the eighth that proved decisive after New York had clawed back to tie it in the sixth. Washington's won two straight after dropping five of seven, a small but meaningful correction for a team that's been frustratingly inconsistent.

• Chris Bassitt [6.2 IP, 1 ER, 7 K | 3.18 PAR] continues to anchor Baltimore's rotation with the kind of consistency they hoped for when they signed him — working deep into the seventh while limiting Houston to one earned run kept the Orioles' bullpen fresh for the nightcap.

The day's results didn't dramatically reshape either league's hierarchy, but they did clarify a few things. Atlanta's loss snapped a stretch where they'd won seven of ten, though at 22-10 they remain comfortably ahead in the NL East with a run differential that suggests they're playing close to their true talent level. The Phillies, meanwhile, have won three straight but remain buried at 12-19 with a Pythagorean record that implies they've been even worse than their actual performance. Detroit's ability to hang around .500 keeps them relevant in a Central division where Cleveland and the Tigers are both 16-16, and the White Sox at 14-17 aren't out of it either. The bigger story might be Houston's continued struggles — at 12-20 with a ghastly run differential, they're the kind of team that either turns it around in May or faces an early summer reckoning about what went wrong.
