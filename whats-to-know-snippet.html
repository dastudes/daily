The American League Central remains the division nobody wants to win, and Saturday did nothing to clarify matters. Detroit and Cleveland came in tied at the top, and the Tigers stayed there by ambushing Jack Leiter [6.2 IP, 5 ER, 10 K | 5.45 ERA, PAR 5] late at Comerica Park — Leiter actually struck out ten Rangers, but Spencer Torkelson [1-4, 1 HR | RC 17]'s two-run shot in the fifth and a four-run seventh sent Texas home. The Guardians, meanwhile, got run over in Sacramento by the Athletics, leaving both Central co-leaders at .514 and the White Sox and Royals lurking within striking distance. Kansas City pulled back to within 2.5 games behind Kris Bubic [7 IP, 1 ER, 7 K | 3.36 ERA, PAR 12], who outdueled Luis Castillo [6 IP, 4 ER, 5 K | 6.33 ERA, PAR 3] in Seattle and dropped the Mariners 2.5 games back in their own division race.

In the National League Central, every team in the division is above .500 — yes, all five — and the day mostly preserved the order. The Cubs stretched their winning streak to five by beating Arizona at Wrigley, while Pittsburgh's Braxton Ashcraft [7.2 IP, 0 ER, 6 K | 3.06 ERA, PAR 14] and Cincinnati's Chase Burns [7 IP, 0 ER, 7 K | 2.20 ERA, PAR 15] combined for 14.2 shutout innings before the Pirates pushed across the only run in the eighth on Oneil Cruz [2-4 | RC 23]'s single. The Reds and Cardinals — St. Louis fell to the Dodgers behind Justin Wrobleski [6 IP, 0 ER, 0 K | 1.25 ERA, PAR 15]'s six scoreless — remain knotted two back of Chicago.

The most dramatic baseball of the day was at Fenway, where Houston and Boston traded zeros for nine innings before a 10th that nearly defied belief. The Astros loaded the bases with nobody out, then watched Jose Altuve [1-4 | RC 17] ground into a home-to-third double play that wiped out two runs — a swing of nearly four-tenths of a win on its own. Cam Smith [3-5 | RC 14] rescued them an at-bat later with a two-run single, though even that came with Brice Matthews [0-3, 1 BB | RC 8] thrown out at the plate after a pair of replay reviews. Boston then put two on with one out in the bottom half before Ceddanne Rafaela [0-4, 1 BB | RC 12] grounded into the game-ending double play. A 5.23 WPA swing is what it looks like when neither team can quite finish the job.

Tampa Bay walked it off against the Giants in their own 10th, with Jonathan Aranda [4-5 | RC 19] — who had four hits — singling home Chandler Simpson [0-5 | RC 15] off Caleb Kilian [1 IP, 0 ER, 0 K | 0.60 ERA, PAR 6]. And up at Target Field, the Blue Jays staged a two-run ninth and brought the tying run to the plate before Lenyn Sosa [0-5 | RC 6] rolled into a 4-6-3 double play to end it; a quieter game than the Fenway epic but a 2.57 WPA swing all the same.

A few performances worth singling out:

  • Chase Burns of the Reds threw seven shutout innings with seven strikeouts at PNC Park and lost, because Braxton Ashcraft was a hair better.
  • Philadelphia's Jesús Luzardo [6.1 IP, 2 ER, 10 K | 5.15 ERA, PAR 9] punched out ten Marlins over 6.1 innings, giving him a share of the NL strikeout lead.
  • The Mets' Mark Vientos [2-4, 2 HR | RC 11] hit two home runs at Angel Stadium, both with Carson Benge [1-2, 2 BB | RC 7] aboard, accounting for four of New York's five runs.
  • Colorado's Mickey Moniak [2-4, 1 BB, 2 HR | RC 27] also hit two home runs and still lost by five — at Coors Field, where Atlanta scored eleven and Spencer Strider [3.1 IP, 3 ER, 6 K | 8.71 ERA, PAR -1] somehow got the no-decision after walking five in 3.1 innings.

Atlanta is now 25-10 and has the best run differential in baseball by a comfortable margin; the NL East is essentially over barring something biblical, with the second-place Marlins 8.5 back. The Yankees similarly continue to bury the AL East. The interesting Pythagorean cases are San Diego (+3.6 wins over expected) and Cincinnati (+5.4), both outperforming their run prevention in ways that tend not to last, while Milwaukee (-3.4) and the Dodgers (-3.1) are the opposite — better than their records suggest. And the Giants, losers of six straight and last in the NL in runs scored, are quietly in free fall.
