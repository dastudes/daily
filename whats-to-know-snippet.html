The Race at the Top

The Brewers keep winning and the Cardinals keep being the team that has to chase them. Milwaukee's whitewashing of St. Louis at American Family Field was the kind of game that quietly tells you who the better team is right now — a five-run fifth inning, a tidy outing from Kyle Harrison [6 IP, 0 ER, 2 K | 1.58 ERA, PAR 23] (today's third-best pitcher by PAR), and not a single Cardinal crossing the plate. Jake Bauers [2-4, 1 HR | RC 29] and Garrett Mitchell [1-4, 1 HR | RC 18] went deep, William Contreras [2-4 | RC 30] doubled in the back-breakers, and the Brewers are now three back in a division where everyone is above .500 and nobody seems capable of stringing together the kind of run that would actually threaten Milwaukee. The Cardinals, meanwhile, have lost three straight and their offense has shown up for none of them.

Out west, the Dodgers and Diamondbacks both won, which keeps the NL West standings exactly where they were — except that Arizona, winners of nine of ten, is suddenly the most dangerous team in the division not named Los Angeles. The Dodgers' 15-6 dismantling of Colorado at Dodger Stadium was less a game than a batting practice session that someone forgot to call off. Mookie Betts [3-5, 2 HR | RC 9] hit two homers, Andy Pages [4-5, 1 HR | RC 38] had four hits, Enrique Hernández [2-2, 1 HR | RC 9] went deep, and even Miguel Rojas [2-5] got into a game on the mound (it went about how you'd expect). Meanwhile at Oracle Park, Arizona's Eduardo Rodriguez [6 IP, 2 ER, 6 K | 2.31 ERA, PAR 22] outlasted Tyler Mahle [5 IP, 3 ER, 3 K | 6.09 ERA, PAR 3] in a back-and-forth affair where Ketel Marte [1-5, 1 HR | RC 32]'s seventh-inning two-run shot proved decisive. The Diamondbacks have closed to four and a half back and are playing like they don't intend to stay there.

The AL East didn't move much — the Rays lost, the Yankees won, and we're still looking at a two-and-a-half-game gap — but the manner of each was notable. Baltimore's Shane Baz [7 IP, 1 ER, 9 K | 4.49 ERA, PAR 12] carved up Tampa Bay at Camden Yards, posting the day's best PAR mark behind nine strikeouts over seven innings, while Samuel Basallo [1-4, 1 HR | RC 24]'s three-run shot in the fifth put the game away. The Yankees, for their part, did not so much beat the Royals as humiliate them, 15-1 at Kauffman, with Amed Rosario [4-6, 2 HR | RC 15] of all people leading the day's batters by linear weights on the strength of a two-homer night. Cam Schlittler [6 IP, 1 ER, 6 K | 1.50 ERA, PAR 34] added another quality start to a season that already has him leading the American League in ERA, PAR, and FIP. He's been the best pitcher in the league, full stop.

One more item worth pausing on: the Cubs have now lost ten in a row. Ten. They're still in the thick of a five-team NL Central scrum where everyone is above .500, which says something about that division, but it also says something about the Cubs, who were pasted 12-1 at PNC Park by a Pittsburgh team that suddenly has a positive run differential and a winning record. Braxton Ashcraft [6.1 IP, 1 ER, 5 K | 2.77 ERA, PAR 23] went into the seventh, Esmerlyn Valdez [1-4, 1 BB, 1 HR | RC 2] and Spencer Horwitz [2-3, 1 HR | RC 28] homered, and the Pirates are quietly the most interesting fifth-place team in baseball.
