Daily Brief

The Boston Red Sox swept a doubleheader from the AL East-leading Tampa Bay Rays, gaining 2 games in the division race, while Baltimore added another 1.5 games on Tampa Bay with a win over Houston. The Rays' rough day tightened the top of the AL East, with the Yankees, Orioles, and Blue Jays all gaining ground despite mixed results.

  • Red Sox swept the Rays in a doubleheader at Fenway, winning 10-0 and 5-3; Jake Bennett [6 IP, 0 ER, 3 K | 2.37 ERA, PAR 20] anchored the shutout and Wilyer Abreu [2-4 | RC 53] homered twice. (BOS)
  • Orioles edged the Astros 3-2, gaining 1.5 games in the AL East race while Houston lost ground in the AL wild card. (ORIOLES)
  • Dodgers beat the Yankees 2-1 at Yankee Stadium, though New York still picked up a half-game in the AL East. (DODGERS)
  • Nationals routed the Athletics 23-4, with Andrés Chaparro [4-5, 1 BB, 2 HR | RC 9] going 4-for-5 with 2 home runs to lead a National League wild card gain. (WSH)
  • Braves crushed the Rangers 15-1 behind seven scoreless innings from Chris Sale [7 IP, 0 ER, 6 K | 2.06 ERA, PAR 42] and a big day from Drake Baldwin [3-4, 1 BB, 1 HR | RC 45]. (ATL)
  • Royals outlasted the Padres 7-6 in the day's most dramatic game by WPA swing, dropping San Diego further back in the NL West. (PADRES)
  • Reds beat the Rockies 7-2 with Elly De La Cruz [4-5, 1 HR | RC 55], Spencer Steer [2-4, 2 HR | RC 46], and Brady Singer [7 IP, 2 ER, 6 K | 4.58 ERA, PAR 11] all contributing; Cincinnati gained a game in the NL wild card race. (CIN)
