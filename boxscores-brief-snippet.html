Daily Brief

The Blue Jays' win over the Rays reshaped the AL East, with Toronto gaining a full game on the division leader while the Yankees, Red Sox, and Orioles all pulled a half-game closer. In the AL Central, both the Tigers and Twins gained ground on the White Sox with wins over Kansas City and Cleveland.

  • Shane Bieber [7 IP, 1 ER, 3 K | 4.77 ERA, PAR 2] and the Blue Jays beat Tampa Bay, cutting the Rays' AL East lead as three other division rivals also gained ground. (TOR)
  • Taj Bradley [7 IP, 1 ER, 5 K | 3.70 ERA, PAR 27] outdueled Gavin Williams [7 IP, 0 ER, 11 K | 3.78 ERA, PAR 32] as the Twins topped the Guardians in the day's most dramatic game by WPA swing, dropping Cleveland further behind the White Sox. (MIN)
  • The Tigers edged the Royals to gain a half-game in the AL Central, with Isaac Collins [1-3, 1 BB, 1 HR | RC 37] homering in the loss. (KC)
  • The Braves held off the Padres, with Ozzie Albies [2-4, 1 HR | RC 55] and Michael Harris II [2-4, 1 HR | RC 61] each homering. (ATL)
  • Tyler Locklear [3-5, 1 HR | RC 4] homered and went 3-for-5 as the Diamondbacks routed the Cardinals, gaining a half-game in the NL West. (AZ)
  • Gavin Williams posted the day's top pitching line in a losing effort, striking out 11 over 7 shutout innings. (CLE)
